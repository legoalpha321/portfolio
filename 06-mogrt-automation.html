The Brief

Make on brand motion something anyone on the team can make.

When Claude Code first came out, it was bad at motion graphics. Raw JSX broke constantly, and Remotion took 5 to 10 minutes to render something that looked off brand and was hard to tweak. For a brand with strict standards and fast turnarounds, that's a nightmare. I wanted AI that could make motion that was fast, stayed on brand, and that I could still go in and tweak by hand.

The Approach

The system handles layout, the AI just picks and writes.

I built 63 templates in After Effects, all MOGRTs and Essential Graphics comps, and cataloged each one by its text style, energy, mood, complexity, duration, and what it's good and bad for. Claude reads that catalog, picks the right templates for a brief or script, and writes the copy. Then a Python script handles the actual layout, timing, and Essential Graphics. The LLM never touches raw animation, so it basically can't go off brand.

The catalog, 63 templates indexed by energy + use

Plain text in, a built sequence out, in 15 seconds.

You can hand it a brief, a script, or just a rough idea, and it gives back a real After Effects or Premiere sequence in under 15 seconds, in 16:9, 9:16, or 1:1. And because everything is shape layers and Essential Graphics, I can still open the comp and tweak anything I want. So it's fast, it stays on brand, and I keep full creative control. Which is kind of the perfect solution.

How It Scales

The bottleneck went from me to no one.

Before this, I was the person everyone came to for custom motion graphics. Now editors on the Developer Channel, Modern Marketing Data Stack, Data Cloud Now, and Predictions series build their own sequences and can't drift off brand. I've watched a dozen plus videos go out that all would have landed in my queue before. It also changed pre production. We can feed it a draft script and get a proof of concept animation back right away, so we can feel out whether the energy and pacing are landing before we commit to building anything.
