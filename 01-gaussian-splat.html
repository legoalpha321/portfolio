Case Study 01 — Frontier Craft

Gaussian Splat Opener

A three minute journey from a single snow particle up to a mountain summit, built from AI stills turned into animated 3D Gaussian splats and rendered to a 143 foot LED wall for the Day 1 keynote.

Output, the Day 1 keynote opener, 3:00.

The Brief

Open the biggest keynote of the year with something nobody had seen before.

The Day 1 opener is the first thing 7,000 people in the room see before the CEO walks out, and it sets the tone for the whole four day conference. They wanted a three minute piece that felt frontier and photorealistic, but still completely art directed. It had to lock 30 days before the show with zero room to break on a 143 foot wall. A live violinist would be playing over it, so the timing had to be exact.

The Approach

Turning a single AI still into a 3D world I could fly through.

It started from pencil storyboards a coworker sketched out. From those I generated the source stills in Nano Banana Pro, so I could dial in the exact color, amount of snow, framing, and camera angle for every scene. Apple SHARP converted each still into a 3D Gaussian splat, and I animated the camera through them in After Effects. Basically it gave me a photorealistic space with real depth that I could move through however I wanted. A normal 2D treatment can't fake that, and AI video generation falls apart at this resolution.

Pencil storyboard → Nano Banana Pro still → 3D Gaussian splat

30 minutes to render, all on a MacBook.

The whole three minute opener rendered in 30 minutes. No render farm, just my laptop. AI video generation would have cost a fortune and still wouldn't have held up on a 143 foot wall. The splat pipeline gave me total camera and color control, it was cheap, and it was fast enough to actually iterate.

AE camera path through the splat field

One throughline the whole room could feel.

The piece moves from a single snow particle, through a forest with a stream running through it, past a flower blooming, into a cave, out to a mountain in the distance, then up the face in a few flyover shots until the sun sets over the summit. This is an AI and data conference, so grounding everyone in something real and natural before all the technical jargon mattered a lot. I scored it with nature sound design too, wind and footsteps in the snow, to really put people there.

On the Wall
Photo, opener running on the 143 ft wall with the live violinist

A live violinist played over it on the 143 foot wall, in front of 7,000 people, with the whole thing livestreamed. It was the palate cleanser right before the CEO walked out.

How It Scales

A new signature technique every year, not a one off.

Every Summit I try to land one new frontier technique that becomes the signature of that keynote. 2025 was the microscopic snowflakes, 2026 was Gaussian splats. And the splat workflow didn't stay a one off. I wired the whole multi step conversion into a single KBar button in After Effects, so anyone on the team can fire it without babysitting the process. A 30 minute render means the look lives in our toolkit now, not behind a render farm.
