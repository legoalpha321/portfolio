Case Study 03 — Frontier Craft

Microscopic Snowflakes

Snowflakes forming like you're watching them under a microscope, except every bit of it is art directed.

Role
Brand Motion Graphics Designer
Year
2025
Scope
Day 2 keynote opener (5 min, live orchestra), CEO keynote moments, and pre and post show graphics, Snowflake Summit
Tools
Midjourney · Trapcode Mir · Runway · Topaz Video · After Effects
The Brief

Make something that feels real and analog in a keynote that's mostly screens and text.

A keynote is hours of bright graphics and text animation. We wanted moments that felt real and analog to break that up, something that looked like actual microscope footage of snowflakes forming. Stock footage of that didn't exist, so I had to come up with a brand new workflow.

The Approach

Building fake microscope footage that holds up as real.

I began by generating snowflake textures in Midjourney, then used Rowbyte's AutoFill plugin to reveal them dynamically. That growing texture drove Trapcode Mir, which gave it real 3D depth so I could place a camera exactly where I wanted. Runway's style transfer applied an icy texture to the otherwise flat, unshaded geometry. And then the secret sauce: I upscaled the clips in Topaz, dropped them to 15fps, added out of focus 3D particles that shifted every frame, and wiggled the exposure to sell the illusion of timelapse photography. A final pass added glow, depth of field bokeh, analog film noise, and color correction. I studied what real microscope footage of ice forming I could find to get the look exactly right.

Pipeline, texture → Mir depth → Runway → grade

Enough control to grow the logo out of the ice.

Because the style transfer was driven by real geometry instead of a raw generation, I had full control over what formed. I grew the ice into the Snowflake logo, right alongside the randomly forming crystals. That's the part you can't get by just asking an AI for microscope footage.

How It Scales

The same idea works on almost anything.

I've reused the core idea well beyond that keynote, a simple animation or piece of 3D geometry driving a fancier 3D render, both on Snowflake Build and quietly inside the 2026 Summit keynote. It's a way of working I keep coming back to, to get the most out of a render.
