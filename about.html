About

Timmy Beckmann

I've been making things move since I was five years old. It started with LEGO stop motion, photos dropped into a Microsoft PowerPoint where I controlled the timing by hitting the spacebar. By 2008 those videos had passed five million views on YouTube, and the ad money bought me my first real camera.

Stop motion taught me that every single part of a film matters. You frame the shot so tightly that you only build what the camera sees. You place every sound effect by hand. I'd bring each frame into Photoshop to paint the VFX, and record my own voiceovers. That was the foundation for everything after it.

I kept making films through middle and high school, taking cues from people like Freddie Wong and the Corridor Crew. I was also rally commissioner in my high school's ASB for two years, which is where I first learned how to run a big live event. That one comes back around every Summit. Then I went to NYU for Film and TV, where I made my own projects but found I got the most out of helping with everyone else's, learning from the people around me.

COVID hit right as I graduated, so I stayed in New York freelancing. I got connected with someone at Snowflake, and the rest is history. I've been a Brand Motion Graphics Designer there since 2021, one of two full time motion designers covering everything from 143 foot keynote openers to social.
