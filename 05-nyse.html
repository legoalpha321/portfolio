The Brief

Turn the entire New York Stock Exchange Snowflake blue, on a two week clock.

October 1, 2025 marked 15 years since Snowflake was founded and 5 years since it went public on the NYSE. To celebrate, we took over the building. That meant a 1.5 minute brand film telling the 15 year story, plus 18 separate deliverables sized for 46 different screens throughout the exchange. The whole thing came together on a quick turn, under two weeks.

The Approach

A 15 year story that doesn't let you breathe.

The film runs about a minute and a half but it's paced like a dense 60 seconds, on purpose. It moves through three acts, the foundation ten years ago, going public five years ago, and where Snowflake is headed with the AI Data Cloud. Driving electronic track, all blue brand palette, kinetic type snapping to the beat, then a live action montage of customers and partners near the end before a hard cut to the logo.

Kinetic type, three-act anniversary film

46 screens, laid out one at a time.

The takeover hit 46 screens across the building, the two Broad Street media walls outside, the boxes above the trading floor booths, the lobby, the conference rooms, the ballroom, and the boardroom. The screens ranged from 1920×1080 all the way to tall verticals like 1255×2580. With the turnaround this tight I laid the content out manually for each one rather than building a rig.

Screen map, 46 sizes across the building
On Site
Photo, the NYSE facade gone Snowflake blue on Broad Street

I flew out for the rollout, which was honestly a highlight. The whole building going Snowflake blue felt great, and I spent part of the day handing out Snowflake blue popsicles to people walking by. When the playback hit a last minute snag, I was right there with my laptop and re rendered the fix just in time.

How It Scales

Built and rolled out in under two weeks.

This one wasn't about a reusable system. With a two week clock I laid all 46 screens out by hand, then flew out to be there for the rollout. Designing a full building takeover and then saving the playback live, on site, is the kind of turnaround the moment actually needed.
